Profile for RWA Election 2019

“Nature does nothing uselessly.”

― Aristotle


Col. Sushil Kumar (Retd)

3-I/ 504

(DU Owner since 2009)



“To avoid criticism say nothing, do nothing, be nothing.”

― Elbert Hubbard


Criticism is unpleasant yet inevitable for the doer. Receiving it constructively is however, a sign of a leader. I thus, hereby urge you all to help me embark on this ordeal where I contribute for the welfare of our society while constructively working on your feedback.

Time is precious. So, I will abstain from the cliche of verbosely mentioning my achievements, thus far, and aspirations, hereon. Hopefully, the candid introduction below will give me the pleasure to make your acquaintance.


I joined the Indian Army after my M Tech from IIT Delhi and was commissioned in the Corps of EME in 1981. I worked in various positions and played a long inning in the Army Aviation Corps to maintain a large fleet of helicopters. I also worked as a Staff Officer in HQ Southern and Western Command and contributed immensely towards forming and implementing the important guidelines and policies pertaining to maintenance of wide range of equipment. Thereafter, I took a premature retirement to join my family business.


Having worked with the top brands in the market, I have rejuvenated myself with the latest trends and practices in security, housekeeping, procurement, budgeting, bidding and technical stipulations.


The proof of the pudding is in the eating. So, I sincerely look forward for an opportunity to serve the Township, and I am very confident that I shall be able to make a worthwhile contribution in improving many facets of RWA functioning, in addition to ensuring noticeable savings in all procurement and AMCs.


Our township is a reflection of our ideologies and standards. I wish to see it prosper so that it receives due admiration and adulation from all visitors alike. Furthermore, it deserves to have a tranquil atmosphere where all issues are resolved amicably with legal option being the last resort. Camaraderie is ingrained in all of us because of the Ethos of Indian Army. Allow me to sign off with the motto of Esprit-de-Corps, "One for all and all for one"!


Cheers,


Col. Sushil Kumar (Retd)
